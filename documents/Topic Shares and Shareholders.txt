Document: Comprehensive Guide to Corporate Capital Structure
Subject: Corporate Finance
Keywords: Debt-to-Equity Ratio, WACC, MM Theory, Capital Structure, Financial Risk, Leverage, Tax Shield

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Capital Structure

Capital Structure refers to the mix of debt, equity, and hybrid securities that a company uses to finance its operations.

--------------------------------------------------

Objective of Capital Structure

The goal of financial management is to determine the Optimal Capital Structure — the point where the value of the firm is maximized while the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) is minimized.

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Optimal Capital Structure

Optimal Capital Structure is the ideal combination of debt and equity that maximizes firm value and minimizes the cost of capital.

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Debt Financing

Debt Financing refers to funds borrowed from external lenders that must be repaid with interest.

Examples of debt financing:
- Corporate bonds
- Bank loans
- Debentures
- Credit facilities

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Equity Financing

Equity Financing refers to funds raised through ownership shares or retained earnings.

Examples of equity financing:
- Common stock
- Preferred stock
- Retained earnings
- Additional paid-in capital

--------------------------------------------------

Formula: Debt-to-Equity Ratio

Debt-to-Equity Ratio = Total Debt / Total Equity

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Financial Leverage

Financial Leverage refers to the use of debt financing to increase the potential return to shareholders.

--------------------------------------------------

Advantages of Debt Financing

- Interest payments are tax-deductible
- Lower cost compared to equity
- No ownership dilution
- Increases return on equity when profits are strong

--------------------------------------------------

Disadvantages of Debt Financing

- Fixed repayment obligation
- Higher financial risk
- Risk of bankruptcy if cash flow becomes unstable
- Increases pressure on company earnings

--------------------------------------------------

Advantages of Equity Financing

- No mandatory repayment
- Lower financial risk
- Improves financial stability
- Better long-term flexibility

--------------------------------------------------

Disadvantages of Equity Financing

- Ownership dilution
- Higher cost compared to debt
- Shareholders require higher returns
- Possible loss of control

--------------------------------------------------

Comparison: Debt Financing vs Equity Financing

Debt Financing:
- Lower cost of capital
- Fixed repayment
- Higher financial risk
- No ownership dilution

Equity Financing:
- Higher cost of capital
- No repayment obligation
- Lower financial risk
- Ownership dilution occurs

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC)

WACC represents the average cost a company pays for its financing from both debt and equity.

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Tax Shield

Tax Shield refers to the reduction in taxable income caused by interest payments on debt.

--------------------------------------------------

Theory: Modigliani-Miller (MM) Theory

The Modigliani-Miller Theory states that in a perfect market, the value of a firm is independent of its capital structure.

--------------------------------------------------

MM Proposition I (No Taxes)

The value of a leveraged firm is equal to the value of an unleveraged firm.

VL = VU

--------------------------------------------------

MM Proposition II (With Corporate Taxes)

The value of a leveraged firm increases because debt creates a tax shield.

VL = VU + (Tax Rate × Debt)

--------------------------------------------------

Theory: Static Trade-Off Theory

The optimal capital structure occurs when the tax benefits of debt are equal to the financial distress costs.

--------------------------------------------------

Theory: Pecking Order Theory

Companies follow a financing hierarchy to reduce information asymmetry.

Order of financing:
1. Internal financing (retained earnings)
2. External debt
3. External equity

--------------------------------------------------

Factors Affecting Capital Structure

- Business risk
- Tax position
- Financial flexibility
- Growth opportunities
- Industry norms

--------------------------------------------------

Definition: Financial Risk

Financial Risk refers to the risk that a company may not be able to meet its debt obligations.
